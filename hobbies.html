Video Game Development

Era of Eternals is an open zone multiplayer fighting game I'm developing solo, where players level up through combat by inflicting damage on opponents. As players progress, they unlock new zones, each offering unique challenges and requiring different strategies. The game features a robust upgrade system, allowing players to enhance abilities, unlock special moves, and access powerful equipment. This combination of progression and customization ensures a dynamic and engaging experience for all players.

Sports

Golf

Growing up, I competed in numerous golf tournaments, participating in both the American Junior Golf Association (AJGA) and the Golf Association of Philadelphia (GAP) tournaments. During my time at The Hill School, I led my golf team to a state championship and multiple league titles, showcasing strong leadership and exceptional skill. Currently, I am a member of the Villanova Club Golf Team and maintain a handicap of +0.3. My ongoing engagement with the sport continues to be a source of personal discipline and competitive achievement.

Squash

I started playing squash in the 8th grade, quickly developing a passion for the game. By my sophomore year at The Hill School, my dedication and rapid improvement earned me a spot on the Varsity squad, where I competed for the next three years. During my time on the team, I worked diligently to refine my skills and strategy, ultimately graduating with a commendable 4.1 rating. This rating reflects not only my technical proficiency but also my deep understanding of the game and commitment to continuous improvement.

Favorite Books


Democracy and Education by John Dewey

Diving into 'Democracy and Education' really opened my eyes to how education shapes our society. John Dewey makes you see that learning isn't just about prepping for your future; it's actually a key part of living a full life right now. His ideas on how education helps us think critically and act responsibly made me appreciate my own educational experiences so much more. It’s crazy how much impact teaching methods and school environments have on us—not just academically but also as citizens.

Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman

Reading 'Thinking, Fast and Slow' really changed the way I look at decision-making. Daniel Kahneman explains how our minds work with two different systems — one fast and intuitive, the other slow and deliberate. It's fascinating and a bit alarming to realize how often I rely on quick judgments instead of taking the time to think things through. This book has made me more aware of my own thought processes and definitely encouraged me to question my gut feelings more often, especially in important decisions.
